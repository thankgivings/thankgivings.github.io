Indigenous peoples in North America have a history of holding communal feasts in celebration of the fall harvest that predates the arrival of European settlers. One of the most influential Indigenous groups in shaping Thanksgiving in Canada was the Haudenosaunee (Iroquois) Confederacy, who practiced their "Thanksgiving Address" or "The Ohen:ton Karihwatehkwen," a ceremonial expression of gratitude for the natural world and the Creator's gifts.
