Artist: Grandma Moses (Anna Mary Robertson Moses; American, 1860–1961) At the ripe age of 78, Mrs. Moses began painting and would come to be known affectionately as “Grandma Moses.” Moses was born into a large, working-class family. She left home at a young age, with minimal education and went to work on a neighboring farm. Catching the turkey—part of the annual Thanksgiving ritual—was one such theme that Moses painted numerous times. Though she often repeated subjects, no two compositions were ever alike. ‘Catching the Thanksgiving Turkey’ was created in 1943 by Grandma Moses in Naïve Art (Primitivism) style.
